## Slide 3

What is Power BI ?
Power BI is a collection of software services, apps, and connectors that work together to turn your unrelated sources of data into coherent, visually immersive, and interactive insights.
Power BI is a business analytics solution with the functionality to visualize data with business intelligence capabilities and share insights across the organization enabling you or your client to make informed decisions quickly.
Power BI dashboards provide a 360-degree view for business users with their most important metrics in one place, updated in real time, and available on all of their devices.
Power BI lets you easily connect to your data sources, visualize and discover what's important, and share that with anyone or everyone you want.

## Slide 4

All companies collect and stores data, and this keeps growing daily. But not all companies are able to tap into their data in order to achive their business potential.
The prime reason is that the data gets stored in various apps, databases, spreadsheets, and third-party platforms, making it challenging to access and report.
Microsoft Power BI has been disrupting the business intelligence market with a variety of features that handle data analytics, visualization, and BI needs.
The more a business expands, the more complex the data becomes, and managing data-driven business decisions becomes crucial.
Microsoft Power BI is the go-to option due to its affordability and extensive features.
By leveraging it, you can organize data efficiently, improve access across the organization, and deliver reliable results.

## Slide 8: Components of Power BI

Components of Power BI
Power Query gives you a graphical user interface to transform data as you need, adding columns, changing types, transformations for date and time, text, and many other operations are available.
Power Query also uses a powerful formula language as code behind called M Code that has many functionalities.
Power Pivot is data modelling engine that provide you a great place to build your star schema, calculated measures and columns, build relationships through entities and so on.

## Slide 23

3. Test & Validate Solution
1. Validate Data Accuracy
As a one-time effort during the migration, you'll need to ensure the data in the new report matches what's displayed in the legacy report.    If there's a difference, be able to explain why. It's more common than you might think to find an error in the legacy solution that gets resolved in the new solution.
As part of ongoing data validation efforts, the new report will typically need to be cross-checked with the original source system.
Ideally, this validation occurs in a repeatable way every time you publish a report change.
2. Validate Security
When validating security, there are two primary aspects to consider:
Data permissions
Access to semantic models, reports, and dashboards
Data permissions are applied by defining row-level security (RLS). It's also possible that data permissions are enforced by the source system when using Direct Query storage mode.
The main ways to grant access to Power BI content are:
Workspace roles
App audience permissions
Sharing an individual report or dashboard

## Slide 24

3. Test & Validate Solution
4. Validate performance
Performance of the Power BI solution is important for consumer experience. Most reports should present visuals in under 10 seconds.
Report performance should be assessed regularly in the Power BI service, in addition to Power BI Desktop.
Many performance issues arise from substandard DAX, poor semantic model design, or suboptimal report design.
Technical environment issues, such as the network, an overloaded data gateway, or how a Premium capacity is configured can also contribute to performance issues.
3. Validate Functionality
Check semantic model details like field names, formatting, sorting, and default summarization behavior.
Verify interactive report features, such as slicers, drill-down actions, drill through actions, expressions, buttons, or bookmarks.
Verify all functionality works as expected in the service, such as the rendering of custom visuals.
Test scheduled refresh, Q&A, and how reports and dashboards look on a mobile device.
