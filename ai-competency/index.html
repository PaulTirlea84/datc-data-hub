    AI Proficiency Framework

    This page reference how every practitioner in Data Engineering and Data Analytics is expected to adopt and grow their AI capability. It covers the proficiency scale, required levels by role, domain-specific guidance, and curated learning paths. Data Engineering builds the foundation. Data Analytics delivers the insight. Both are essential and both must evolve with AI.

    AI Proficiency Roadmap

    The platform follows a continuous improvement cycle across five objectives:

    Objective What It Means
    Increase AI proficiency Move practitioners from Assisted → Agentic → Integrated → Governed across both domains
    Standardize AI usage Establish consistent AI practices across Data Engineering and Data Analytics roles
    Adopt responsibly Roll out Copilot, LLMs, and RAG on Snowflake, AWS, Azure, and GCP with proper validation
    Enable AI-ready platforms Build data platforms that power AI-driven delivery in Power BI and Tableau
    Ensure governance Maintain governance, cost control, and reuse of AI patterns across the practice

    AI Proficiency Scale

    All AI competency requirements use a five-level proficiency scale aligned to Accenture's FY26 AI Data Platform Strategy. Requirements are cumulative — each level includes all previous proficiency levels.

    Proficiency Level Proficiency Name What It Means
    P1 AI Assisted Use built-in AI features (Copilot, suggestions, recommendations) to support your day-to-day work. Follow existing prompts and validate outputs with guidance.
    P2 AI Accelerated Actively use LLMs to speed up your tasks. Validate AI outputs independently and adapt prompts for better results. AI meaningfully reduces the time for standard work.
    P3 Agentic AI Design and execute multi-step AI workflows. Build reusable prompt structures, apply RAG patterns where relevant, and guide peers on effective AI use.
    P4 Integrated AI Embed AI capabilities directly into pipelines, dashboards, and team workflows at project level. Create frameworks and lead AI adoption on your engagements.
    P5 Governed AI Define standards, governance, and strategy for AI use across the practice. Advise leadership and shape how AI evolves in the organisation.

    Responsible AI

    Validation is mandatory at every level

    At all proficiency levels, AI-generated outputs must be validated before use in any deliverable.

    • Check AI-generated code or logic for correctness against the business requirement
    • Do not publish, deploy, or share AI-generated results without independent review
    • Flag and escalate unexpected, incorrect, or biased outputs
    • Apply Accenture's Responsible AI principles in all AI-assisted work Document which parts of a deliverable were AI-assisted when working in regulated environments

    P1–P2, validation happens with senior support. · P3 onward, independent validation is expected as standard practice. · P4 onward, you are also responsible for defining what validation looks like for your team.
