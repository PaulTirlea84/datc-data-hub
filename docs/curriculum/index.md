# Curriculum Overview

The **DEVI curriculum** is the structured learning framework for the Data Engineering & Visual Insights (DEVI) practice within DATC Bucharest. It maps Accenture's global Data & AI skills catalog to the two domain tracks, defines what "good" looks like at every career level, and gives practitioners and their managers a shared language for growth conversations.

---

## Two Domain Tracks

### Visual Insights

Front-end data delivery. Practitioners design and build dashboards, reports, and insight interfaces that drive adoption and decision-making. The primary tool is Microsoft Power BI, supplemented by the broader BI ecosystem (Qlik, Looker, Grafana, D3.js, SSAS, IBM Cognos).

Accenture specializations covered: BI Engineering, Data Visualization, Enterprise BI Architecture & Engineering, Vis & Interaction Science.

### Data Engineering

Back-end data infrastructure. Practitioners build the pipelines, platforms, and governance structures that feed the Visual Insights layer and client applications with clean, reliable data. The stack spans Spark, Kafka, Airflow, Snowflake, Databricks, Azure Synapse, AWS Glue, BigQuery, and more.

Accenture specializations covered: Data Engineering, Data Architecture, Databricks, Modern Data Management & Governance.

## Proficiency Scale

All competency targets in this curriculum use a six-level proficiency scale aligned to Accenture's global skills framework.

| Code | Label | What it means in practice |
|------|-------|--------------------------|
| P1 | Awareness | You know the concept exists and can describe it at a high level. No hands-on experience required. |
| P2 | Foundational | You have completed structured learning and can apply the skill with close supervision on well-defined tasks. |
| P3 | Practitioner | You apply the skill independently on standard project work. You can explain your approach and troubleshoot common issues. |
| P4 | Advanced | You handle complex, non-standard situations. You guide others and contribute to technical decisions in this area. |
| P5 | Expert | You set the standard for the practice. You design approaches, resolve the hardest problems, and are recognized across projects. |
| P6 | Leader | You shape strategy and build capability at scale. You define how the discipline evolves within the organization. |

## Level Progression

The career ladder in DEVI runs from L12 (entry) through L8 (Associate Manager / Architect). Each promotion step shifts the balance from supported execution toward independent ownership, then toward leadership.

| Step | Role | Career Level | Focus shift |
|------|------|-------------|-------------|
| Entry | Associate | L12 | Learning fundamentals under supervision. Executes defined tasks within existing solutions. |
| +1 | Analyst | L11 | Increasing independence. Owns discrete workstreams. Starts to review peers' work. |
| +2 | Senior Analyst | L10 | Full independent delivery. Drives design decisions within their workstream. Mentors Associates. |
| +3 | Specialist | L9 | Cross-project scope. Sets technical direction within a domain. Owns quality standards. |
| +4 | Associate Manager | L8 | People and delivery leadership. Manages a team, drives staffing decisions, owns client relationships. |
| Bridge | BI Architect / Data Architect | L9 Bridge | Deep technical specialization. Architect track for practitioners who deepen expertise rather than move into people management. |
| Bridge | Lead BI Architect / Lead Data Architect | L8 Bridge | Principal technical voice for the practice. Shapes offerings, standards, and capability growth. |

## How to Use This Curriculum

### Self-assessment

Navigate to the competency map for your domain. Find your current role column. For each competency, rate yourself honestly against the P1–P6 scale. Flag the gaps between your current level and the target for your role — those become your priority learning areas for the next cycle.

### Manager conversations

Bring your self-assessment to your next career development check-in. Use the competency targets as a concrete frame: "I am at P2 on data modeling but the target for Senior Analyst is P3. Here is my plan to close it." This replaces vague growth conversations with a shared, actionable picture that both sides can track.

### Learning planning

Use the role-level pages under each domain (for example, Visual Insights → Senior Analyst) to find recommended resources for each competency. The [Assets & Resources Registry](../assets/registry.md) lists learning platforms, certifications, and internal documents mapped to specific skill areas.

### Promotion readiness

When assessing readiness to move from one level to the next, check that you have reached the target proficiency across all core competencies for your current level — not just your strongest areas. Breadth matters as much as depth at L12–L10. Deep technical authority and the ability to guide others are the key differentiators at L9 and above.
