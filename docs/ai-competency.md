## AI Proficiency

### Proficiency Scale

All AI competency requirements use a five-level proficiency scale aligned to Accenture's FY26 AI Data Platform Strategy. Requirements are **cumulative** — each level includes all previous proficiency levels.

| Proficiency Level | Proficiency Name | What It Means |
|:---:|:---:|---|
| **P1** | **AI Assisted** | You use built-in AI features (Copilot, suggestions, recommendations) to support your day-to-day work. You follow existing prompts and validate outputs with guidance. |
| **P2** | **AI Accelerated** | You actively use LLMs to speed up your tasks. You validate AI outputs independently and adapt prompts for better results. AI meaningfully reduces the time for standard work. |
| **P3** | **Agentic AI** | You design and execute multi-step AI workflows. You build reusable prompt structures, apply RAG patterns where relevant, and guide peers on effective AI use. |
| **P4** | **Integrated AI** | You embed AI capabilities directly into pipelines, dashboards, and team workflows at project level. You create frameworks and lead AI adoption on your engagements. |
| **P5** | **Governed AI** | You define standards, governance, and strategy for AI use across the practice. You advise leadership and shape how AI evolves in the organisation. |

## Data Engineering

### AI Competency

AI-enabled Data Engineers prepare, process, and manage data for analytics, reporting, and AI applications. They leverage AI tools to accelerate transformations, ensure data quality, build scalable pipelines, and create the data foundations that power GenAI solutions — including RAG, embedding pipelines, and semantic search.

**Primary AI tools:** GitHub Copilot · Snowflake Cortex · Azure data services · AWS / GCP AI tools


???+ note "P1 — AI Assisted"

    Uses LLMs to generate simple SQL and Python transformation code. Remediates data issues with guidance from seniors. Monitors and maintains existing pipelines. Learns fundamentals of vector databases and embeddings. Validates AI-generated code with support before applying it.

???+ note "P2 — AI Accelerated"

    Leverages AI tools to automate transformations, validations, and data ingestion tasks. Builds simple pipelines for new data sources independently. Begins working with vector databases and embedding pipelines. Uses Snowflake Cortex or GitHub Copilot as a regular part of development. Validates AI outputs independently.

???+ note "P3 — Agentic AI"

    Designs and executes multi-step agentic data workflows. Builds complex pipelines for structured and unstructured data. Constructs RAG-ready data pipelines: chunking, embedding generation, metadata enrichment. Ensures data is validated, governed, and AI-compliant for downstream AI teams. Coaches peers on AI-augmented engineering.

???+ note "P4 — Integrated AI"

    Leads architecture design for major AI data initiatives — RAG platforms, semantic layers, AI data products. Optimises pipelines for retrieval performance, efficiency, and scalability. Embeds AI tooling as a standard part of team delivery. Creates reusable automation frameworks and engineering best practices.

???+ note "P5 — Governed AI"

    Defines enterprise-wide AI data engineering strategy and standards. Shapes the architecture, governance, and data quality vision across the organisation. Acts as the recognised authority on engineering for AI and RAG. Advises leadership and drives AI data ecosystem transformation at scale.

### AI Role Details

Builds and governs the data infrastructure that powers AI — pipelines, cloud platforms, semantic layers, and RAG-ready data foundations.

???+ note "AI Data Engineering"

    - **Associate** — foundational SQL, Python, and pipeline work under guidance · P1
    - **Analyst** — independent ETL delivery, data quality ownership, AI-assisted development · P1–P2
    - **Senior Analyst** — owns complex pipelines end-to-end, introduces AI tooling into workflows · P2–P3
    - **Specialist** — deep expert in cloud architecture and DWH, sets engineering standards · P3
    - **Associate Manager** — leads the engineering stream, owns delivery and platform decisions · P3–P4
    - **Manager** — sets technical direction, drives AI adoption strategy, owns domain standards · P4
    - **Architect** — defines enterprise AI data engineering strategy and standards; shapes the vision for data architecture, governance, and quality · P5

## Data Analytics

### AI Competency

AI-enabled Analytics practitioners enhance reporting, insight generation, and decision support by leveraging AI and LLM tools. They combine traditional BI methods with AI-assisted data transformation, KPI creation, anomaly detection, and automated storytelling.

**Primary AI tools:** Power BI with Copilot / Claude · Fabric Copilot · Tableau Cloud with AI · AWS QuickSight with Claude


???+ note "P1 — AI Assisted"

    Uses AI tools (Copilot, LLMs) for basic tasks: summarising data, exploring datasets, generating simple transformation code (DAX, SQL, M), getting layout suggestions, and drafting KPI definitions. Follows pre-built prompts and templates. Validates outputs with senior guidance before applying.

???+ note "P2 — AI Accelerated"

    Uses LLMs to speed up reporting work: KPI calculations, dashboard logic, layout improvements, insight generation. Validates AI-generated findings independently. Begins adapting prompts for accuracy and consistency. Automates repetitive analytical tasks. AI meaningfully reduces time on standard report development.

???+ note "P3 — Agentic AI"

    Designs and uses agentic AI workflows to automate multi-step analytical tasks (transform → calculate → summarise → narrate). Creates reusable prompt patterns for repeatable analytical processes. Applies AI-assisted techniques: anomaly detection, forecasting, clustering, automated insights. Guides peers on effective AI use in reporting.

???+ note "P4 — Integrated AI"

    Embeds AI logic directly into dashboards, KPI frameworks, and semantic data models. Builds scalable frameworks and reusable prompt libraries for the analytics team. Leads AI adoption practices across projects. Collaborates with Data Engineering on AI-powered data pipelines that feed reporting and insight layers.

???+ note "P5 — Governed AI"

    Defines enterprise-wide AI analytics practices and governance. Advises leadership on AI opportunities in decision-making and reporting. Shapes standards and best practices for responsible AI use in analytics. Guides teams organisation-wide on integrating AI-driven insights into business workflows.

### AI Role Details

Builds and governs the reporting and insight layer — dashboards, semantic models, KPI frameworks, and AI-driven analytics.

???+ note "Data Analytics"


    - **Associate** — foundational Power BI and reporting work under guidance · P1
    - **Analyst** — independent report delivery, DAX and M ownership, AI-assisted development · P1–P2
    - **Senior Analyst** — owns report lifecycle end-to-end, introduces AI tooling into workflows · P2–P3
    - **Specialist** — deep expert in BI architecture and analytics, sets reporting standards · P3
    - **Associate Manager** — leads the analytics stream, owns delivery and platform decisions · P3–P4
    - **Manager** — sets analytical direction, drives AI adoption strategy, owns domain standards · P4
    - **Architect** — defines enterprise-wide AI analytics practices and data-to-insight strategy; advises leadership on AI opportunities · P5

## Responsible AI

!!! warning "Validation is mandatory at every level"
    At all proficiency levels, AI-generated outputs must be **validated before use in any deliverable**.

- Check AI-generated code or logic for correctness against the business requirement
- Do not publish, deploy, or share AI-generated results without independent review
- Flag and escalate unexpected, incorrect, or biased outputs
- Apply Accenture's Responsible AI principles in all AI-assisted work

At P1–P2, validation happens with senior support. From P3 onward, independent validation is expected as standard practice.
