# Associate Manager — Data Engineering

> **Career Level:** L8 | **Domain:** Data Engineering

## Overview

An Associate Manager in Data Engineering leads a team of engineers to deliver data platform solutions to clients. They hold accountability for delivery quality, timeline, and team performance — balancing technical oversight with people management and client stakeholder engagement. While they remain technically credible and capable, their primary focus shifts from hands-on coding to planning, unblocking the team, quality assurance, and representing the data engineering capability in client conversations and internal forums.

## Learning Goals

- Own delivery of a data engineering workstream or small project end-to-end
- Manage a team of 3–8 engineers: task planning, code review oversight, performance feedback
- Translate client business requirements into technical backlog and sprint plans
- Drive data platform strategy decisions within the project (cloud selection, toolchain, standards)
- Oversee data architecture decisions and validate them against project constraints
- Build and maintain client relationships at a working level (daily standups, status reporting, change management)
- Contribute to pre-sales activities: estimation, proposal writing, solution design
- Champion quality and engineering standards across the team (code review culture, documentation, testing)
- Develop the careers of junior team members through structured mentoring

## Key Skills

**Technical Skills** (from Data Engineering, Data Architecture, and Modern Data Management specializations)
- Cloud Data Architecture (Azure, AWS, GCP — advanced working knowledge)
- Big Data Engineering (Spark, Databricks, Kafka) — oversight and quality assurance level
- Data Architecture Implementation and Test
- Data Governance Operating Model
- Enterprise Data Management
- Continuous Integration (CI) Tools and DevOps at scale
- Data Warehouse Tools and performance management
- Infrastructure as Code (Terraform) — review and approval level

**Soft Skills**
- Team leadership and people management (goal setting, performance reviews)
- Client stakeholder management and escalation handling
- Project planning — backlog grooming, sprint planning, release management
- Executive communication: written summaries, status decks, risk registers
- Estimation and commercial awareness (effort/cost modelling)
- Conflict resolution and cross-team coordination
