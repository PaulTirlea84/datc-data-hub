# AI Competency — Data Platform

## About DATA Platform

The **Data Platform** is Accenture Bucharest's delivery capability within the global **Data & AI** practice — part of Danubia ATC, a center with **119 specialists** (76 Data Engineering + 43 Data Analytics) across the two domains. Bucharest contributes **41 people** (34 DE + 7 DA). We cover the full data value chain from raw ingestion through to decision-ready insights, serving clients across industries including healthcare, telco, finance, and consumer goods.

The platform operates across two core domains:

## AI Integration

Integrating AI into our work is not optional — it is the direction of the platform. Every practitioner in the Data Platform is expected to progressively adopt AI tools within their existing domain. This is not about building AI models. It is about **using AI effectively to do your current job better**:

- **Engineering** practitioners use AI to write, test, and optimize pipelines faster, automate data quality checks, and build data layers that power AI applications (RAG, embeddings, semantic search)
- **Data Analytics** practitioners use AI to accelerate reporting, generate KPIs, detect anomalies, automate insights, and build smarter dashboards

The platforms in scope are:

| Domain | Primary AI Tools |
|---|---|
| **Data Analytics** | Power BI with Copilot / Claude · Tableau Cloud with AI · AWS QuickSight with Claude · Fabric Copilot |
| **Engineering** | Snowflake Cortex · GitHub Copilot · Azure AI-ready data services · AWS / GCP AI tools |

## AI Data Engineering — AI Competency

AI-enabled Data Engineers prepare, process, and manage data for analytics, reporting, and AI applications. They leverage AI tools to accelerate transformations, ensure data quality, build scalable pipelines, and create the data foundations that power GenAI solutions — including Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG), embedding pipelines, and semantic search.

**Primary AI tools:** GitHub Copilot · Snowflake Cortex · Azure data services · AWS / GCP AI tools

---

### P1 — AI Assisted

Uses LLMs to generate simple SQL and Python transformation code. Remediates data issues with guidance from seniors. Monitors and maintains existing pipelines. Learns fundamentals of vector databases and embeddings. Validates AI-generated code with support before applying it.

### P2 — AI Accelerated

Leverages AI tools to automate transformations, validations, and data ingestion tasks. Builds simple pipelines for new data sources independently. Begins working with vector databases and embedding pipelines. Uses Snowflake Cortex or GitHub Copilot as a regular part of development work. Validates AI outputs independently.

### P3 — Agentic AI

Designs and executes multi-step agentic data workflows. Builds complex pipelines for structured and unstructured data. Constructs RAG-ready data pipelines: chunking, embedding generation, metadata enrichment. Ensures data is validated, governed, and AI-compliant for downstream AI teams. Coaches peers on AI-augmented engineering.

### P4 — Integrated AI

Leads architecture design for major AI data initiatives — RAG platforms, semantic layers, AI data products. Optimizes pipelines for retrieval performance, efficiency, and scalability. Embeds AI tooling as a standard part of team delivery. Creates reusable automation frameworks and engineering best practices. Mentors junior engineers on AI-augmented workflows.

### P5 — Governed AI

Defines enterprise-wide AI data engineering strategy and standards. Shapes the architecture, governance, and data quality vision across the organization. Acts as the recognized authority on engineering for AI and RAG. Advises leadership and drives AI data ecosystem transformation at scale.

## AI Data Analytics — AI Competency

AI-enabled Analytics practitioners enhance reporting, insight generation, and decision support by leveraging AI and LLM tools. They combine traditional BI methods with AI-assisted data transformation, KPI creation, anomaly detection, and automated storytelling. The role accelerates analytical tasks, improves accuracy, and enables smarter decision-making across the organization.

**Primary AI tools:** Power BI with Copilot / Claude · Fabric Copilot · Tableau Cloud with AI · AWS QuickSight with Claude

---

### P1 — AI Assisted

Uses AI tools (Copilot, LLMs) for basic tasks: summarizing data, exploring datasets, generating simple transformation code (DAX, SQL, M), getting layout suggestions, and drafting KPI definitions. Follows pre-built prompts and templates. Validates outputs with senior guidance before applying.

### P2 — AI Accelerated

Uses LLMs to speed up reporting work: KPI calculations, dashboard logic, layout improvements, insight generation. Validates AI-generated findings independently. Begins adapting prompts for accuracy and consistency. Automates repetitive analytical tasks. AI meaningfully reduces time on standard report development.

### P3 — Agentic AI

Designs and uses agentic AI workflows to automate multi-step analytical tasks (transform → calculate → summarize → narrate). Creates reusable prompt patterns for repeatable analytical processes. Applies AI-assisted techniques: anomaly detection, forecasting, clustering, automated insights. Guides and supports peers on effective AI use in reporting.

### P4 — Integrated AI

Embeds AI logic directly into dashboards, KPI frameworks, and semantic data models. Builds scalable frameworks and reusable prompt libraries for the analytics team. Leads AI adoption practices across projects. Collaborates with Data Engineering on AI-powered data pipelines that feed reporting and insight layers.

### P5 — Governed AI

Defines enterprise-wide AI analytics practices and governance. Advises leadership on AI opportunities in decision-making and reporting. Shapes standards and best practices for responsible AI use in analytics. Guides teams organization-wide on integrating AI-driven insights into business workflows.

## Responsible AI

At all proficiency levels, AI-generated outputs must be **validated before use in any deliverable**:

- Check AI-generated code or logic for correctness against the business requirement
- Do not publish, deploy, or share AI-generated results without independent review
- Flag and escalate unexpected, incorrect, or biased outputs
- Apply Accenture's Responsible AI principles in all AI-assisted work

At P1–P2, validation happens with senior support. From P3 onward, independent validation is expected as standard practice.
